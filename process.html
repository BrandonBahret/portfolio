Introduction - Before we get started

Creating a new website might seem daunting but doesn’t need to be. New endeavors often appear daunting at first, but with a comprehensive plan even daunting tasks can seem exciting.

stage 1:

Design

Design - The start of all great things

building the project brief

First, we need to determine what goals you aim to achieve with your new website. This first step is to ensure a new website will be a genuine investment in your business, not a mere expense. Based on your goals, we will identify the content your site requires, rank that content’s importance, and conceptualize special features that could improve your business’s efficiency.

After we know what your site needs to say, then we consider how to say it. In other words, we need to define the style & tone your website should express. This information will guide the design process with the intent to more authentically represent your brand.

information architecture & wireframes

sitemap: Site Structuring is where the information gathered in our dialogues begin to take form. Taking all the necessary content in mind, a sitemap will be architected with the intent of later creating a fluid, intuitive user experience for your site. Essentially, a sitemap is a graph which describes how a website’s pages should link between each other; additional information can be added to document what content will appear on each page.

wireframing: A wireframe is essentially the scaffolding of a user interface. Without applying stylization, possible layouts are explored to decide how to structure your content on each page.

creating mock-ups

This is when everything comes together. Taking influence from established style preferences, sitemap and wireframes, we create realistic mockups of your site. These mockups will illustrate how your website will look when displayed on various screen sizes. After approval of a site mockup, the development of your site begins.

stage 2:

Develop

Development - Where the value of design is realized

front-end development

Development comes in two phases. Front-end development is the coding of the site’s interface. Rest assured, your site will be tested to display properly using various devices and browers.

back-end development

Back-end development is the programming required for the site’s logic, functionality, and content management. Throughout the entire process I will write and execute unit-tests which help safeguard against leaving bugs undiscovered. For most projects, front-end and back-end development should take fewer than two weeks. The result will be a secure, efficient, authentic recreation of the agreed upon site mockup.

stage 3:

Deploy

Deployment - Finding a home

deployment & support

Huzzah! The site is now finished and ready to be deployed to the internet. I will make recommendations for hosting services that provide Infrastructure as a Service (IaaS) that will provide a secure place for your site to live. You should anticipate annual recurring costs for domain registration ($12), hosting ($36), security certificates ($25) which typically total to $75 annually. I will provide training on how to manage your website's content and offer support for any future development you may require.
