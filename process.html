Introduction - Before we get started

Creating software might seem daunting — new endeavors often do — but with a plan this can become an exciting adventure!

stage 1:

Design

Design - The start of great things

building the project brief

First, we need to determine what goals you aim to achieve with your new website or app. This first step ensures the project is a genuine investment, instead of a mere expense. Based on your goals, we will identify the content and features your project needs.

Once we understand what the content will be, we'll need to define the style & tone the project should embody. We'll create style guides for color, writing, layout, and so on. These choices will ultimately define how your audience experiences the final product.

Consider this a free consultation period after which we'll draft and enter into a service contract.

information architecture

Key to any thoughtful product is good information architecture (IA).

As an example, consider resturant menus: we've all had the experience of reading a bad menu; there's no clearly defined categories, text is tightly packed and unorganized — it's just plain hard to read. That's what bad IA does to a user's experience.

Good IA on the web starts with a sitemap. Essentially, a sitemap is a graph that describes how web pages connect between each other. Consideration will be spent dividing the content in our brief into categories (pages), then those categories will be connected into a graph.

wireframes

A wireframe is the scaffolding of a user interface. Without applying stylization (e.g. color, font, shape), possible layouts will be explored to find how content should be structured on each page. It's at this phase where layout changes are the most affordable effort-wise. When we're wireframing we're just drawing lines on a page, so it's crucial that design iterations happen here instead of later.

creating mock-ups

This is when everything comes together. From our wireframes & style guides, mockups will be created that illustrate expectations for the final product. Before development begins, you'll have the opportunity to review an interactive mockup (like this one) of your project-to-be.

stage 2:

Develop

Development - Where designs are realized

front-end development

This phase of development is about replicating our mockup as faithfully as possible in code. Why isn't pixel-perfection the goal? Because that would be a never-ending process. Our designs almost always need to scale to undiscernible number of monitor sizes, and so we can only ever strive for faithfullness. In other words, mockups are like directives (something to tend towards) instead of demands (rigid and absolute.)

The technologies used to build the front-end depends on the project.

back-end development

This is the "brains" of the project. Any dynamic features that we've planned are implemnted at this stage.

stage 3:

Deploy

Deployment - Finding a home

deployment & support

Awesome, the project is complete! All that's left is to make everything available to your audience.

For websites,
I'll make recommendations for hosting services that provide the secure infrastructure we need. Once a vendor is selected, I'll set everything up as neccessary. I will provide training on how to manage your website's content.

For apps,
I'll assist in getting your app listed in Google Play * and App Store. If needed, I'll make recommendations for hosting services that provide secure infrastructure for the app's back-end. I'll provide training on how to manage your app's content.

* I can't build applications for Apple devices. Apple forces developers to buy their computers (which I cannot afford) to make software for their devices.
